## The 6 forces that hold a viewer
1. **The Hook (0–15s)** decides 80% of the outcome. Open on the single most provocative line +
   biggest number. No logo intro, no throat-clearing. Then plant an **open loop**: tease the
   destination ("…the men who never appear in the building at all") so they must reach the end.
2. **Escalation** — every level must out-do the last on THREE axes simultaneously:
   money (225K → 390K → 3M → 5.8M → 14M → 2.4B), power (clients → firms → governments),
   and morality (earnest → compromise → cold → hollow). Rising numbers = dopamine + dread.
3. **Pacing / pattern interrupts** — new scene every ~12–22s; alternate scene *type* (desk →
   tower → boardroom → jet → war room); punctuate with on-screen numbers; let the **face change**.
   Kills the mid-video slump (insert a striking line/visual around the 50–60% mark).
4. **Micro-arc per level** — setup → telling detail → cost. Each level is a small story with a price.
5. **Emotional throughline** — the hardening arc carries the whole runtime; it's the real "plot."
6. **Payoff + loop** — the ending must *pay off the open loop* (the Architect) and **echo the opening**
   (a new young associate walks in) → cyclical, rewatchable, shareable. End on a quotable line.

## Concrete rules for this series
- **Specificity = credibility = watch time.** Real comp figures, real deal mechanics (high-yield
  issuance, leveraged buyout, right-sizing). Vague = skip.
- **Second person, cold, present tense.** "You" implicates the viewer; present tense = immediacy.
- **Numbers as visual beats.** Each level reveals a number on screen — the spine of the escalation.
- **One idea per scene.** Narration ~60–95 words/scene; ~20 scenes ≈ 12–13 min.
- **Audio:** crisp VO dominant; music swells on level transitions; silence before the biggest lines.
- **CTA:** never in the first half. Place a soft subscribe beat after a peak (~level 6) or at the very
  end; keep it out of the immersion. (We put the hard CTA in the description.)
- **Title/thumbnail synergy** per PACKAGING_PLAYBOOK.md — complement, don't repeat.

## Retention checklist (gate before publish)
- [ ] First line lands in <3s and contains a number or a threat.
- [ ] Open loop planted in intro, paid off at end.
- [ ] Every level's number is bigger than the last.
- [ ] No two adjacent scenes share the same background type.
- [ ] A re-hook (striking visual/line) near the midpoint.
- [ ] Final line is quotable + the opening image returns (loop).
