# Research — Every Level of a Hedge Fund Manager
(Hybrid: model knowledge of the hedge-fund career + comp, with key numbers web-verified June 2026.
Comp figures are RANGES that swing wildly by fund size, strategy, and year — treat as representative,
not precise. **VERIFY** flags note the softest numbers.)

## The spine on screen: THE BOOK YOU CONTROL (power) + your personal take (sub-caption)
A hedge fund's real escalation is not salary — it's the size of the capital you are trusted to move.
So the big gold number is the **book / AUM you command**; the **sub-caption** carries your personal
pay/carry. This mirrors the founder ep's valuation-vs-personal spine: the gap between what you MOVE
and what you KEEP is the drama. Levels 1–2 (no book) lead with comp; from Level 3 the spine becomes
the book and pay drops to the sub.

## The 8-level ladder + numbers
1. **Junior Analyst** — cover a sector, build models, pitch longs/shorts to a PM. You control **$0**
   of capital; you only have ideas. All-in **~$200–300K** at a solid single-manager/pod fund (base
   caps ~$200K; bonus is the rest). Overlay: **$0 book · ~$250K pay**.
2. **Senior Analyst** — your best ideas get *sized*; the PM puts real money behind your conviction and
   your "sleeve" P&L is tracked by name. Still not your book. All-in **~$500K–$1M**. Overlay: **~$600K**.
3. **Portfolio Manager (your own book)** — the platform hands you a book — say **$300M** — and a
   contract: you keep roughly **10–15% of the net P&L** you generate (the "payout %"). A good year
   (~8% on $300M ≈ $24M P&L, ~15% ≈ ~$3M) → **~$3M**. Overlay: **$300,000,000 book**.
4. **Senior / Star PM** — your book grows toward **$1B+** (a $2B pod allocation is common at the
   biggest multimanagers); a strong year's bonus pool can reach **~$50M**, you take the bulk →
   **~$15M**. You are a franchise the platform protects. Overlay: **$1,000,000,000 book**.
5. **Launch Your Own Fund** — you raise outside capital, **~$1.5B AUM**, and charge **"2 and 20"**:
   2% management fee + 20% of profits. You are now the principal; a good year → **~$50M** to you.
   Overlay: **$1,500,000,000 AUM · 2 AND 20**.
6. **The Multi-Billion Fund** — you scale to **~$15B AUM**; pensions and endowments are your LPs.
   2% of $15B = **$300M/yr in management fees ALONE**, before a dollar of performance. Personal
   **$200M+**. Overlay: **$15,000,000,000 AUM**.
7. **The Titan / Market Mover** — **$50B+** (Citadel, Millennium, Bridgewater scale). Your positions
   move markets; you can break a currency (Soros vs. the Bank of England, 1992, ~$1B profit in days);
   finance ministers take your call. You are a **billionaire**. Overlay: **$50,000,000,000**.
8. **The Allocators above all funds (the "Architect" beat)** — the capital that decides which managers
   get money at all: pensions, sovereign-wealth funds, fund-of-funds, the LPs. The whole industry is
   **~$5 trillion** (record ~$4.98T in Q3 2025). They don't trade. They decide who *gets* to trade.
   Overlay: **$5,000,000,000,000**.

## Real mechanics to name (specificity = credibility)
the book / the sleeve, P&L, long/short, the pitch, position sizing, the Sharpe ratio, drawdown,
the risk limit / getting "stopped out", the pod / multimanager platform (Citadel, Millennium, Point72),
2-and-20, the management fee vs. the performance fee, carried interest, high-water mark, AUM, LPs
(pensions, endowments, sovereign wealth), the prime broker, leverage, redemptions / gates, the
Bloomberg terminal, the macro trade, "breaking the Bank of England", the lock-up.

## Emotional arc (the hardening, finance flavor)
earnest idea-machine → first time you're told to size a position you're not sure about → cold P&L
animal (you are your last trade) → franchise PM who stops feeling the losses → principal who trades
other people's pensions → institution that moves markets → the allocator who feels nothing when a
position makes $380M in a week and a rival fund gets gated to zero.
