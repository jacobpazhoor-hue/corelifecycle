## The spine on screen: ASSETS YOU CONTROL (not what you own)
Real estate is a **leverage** story: you control huge assets with a thin slice of equity (debt +
other people's money). So the big on-screen number is **YOU CONTROL $X** (the asset value you command),
with your personal cut / equity / down payment in the gold SUB-caption — sharpening the gap between
what you *control* and what you actually *own*. Monotonic escalation:
**$0 → ~$300K → ~$4M → ~$40M → ~$250M → ~$2B → ~$600B → $393T** (the whole asset class).

The moral throughline is a single word decaying: **home → housing → unit → door → asset → yield**.
The thing humans need to live becomes a line in a spreadsheet.

## The 8-level ladder
1. **The Agent** (entry) — you own *nothing*; you broker other people's homes for a commission,
   ~5–6% split between both sides' brokers (~3% your side, then split with your broker). Median
   REALTOR® gross income ~**$56K** (NAR 2023: $56,400; up to ~$58K), but new agents starve: **62% of
   agents with ≤2 yrs experience made under $10K**, median for that group ~**$8,100**. You sell the
   dream of home. Overlay: **YOU CONTROL $0 · MEDIAN ~$56K**.
2. **The Landlord** — buy your first rental (house-hack a duplex / FHA 3.5% down or 20% down). Control
   ~**$300K** of property on ~**$60K** down — leverage. Cash flow a few hundred a month per unit; the
   tenant pays your mortgage. The first **eviction** is the first moral gray zone. Overlay:
   **YOU CONTROL ~$300K · ~$60K DOWN**.
3. **The Portfolio Investor** — **BRRRR** (Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat): pull your equity back
   out and redeploy. 20–40 "doors," a small multifamily. Control ~**$4M**, your equity maybe ~$800K.
   You stop calling them homes; they are *units* and *doors*. Overlay: **YOU CONTROL ~$4M · 30 DOORS**.
4. **The Syndicator** — stop using your own money. Raise **LP capital** to buy a 200-unit apartment
   complex. Control ~**$40M** with ~$0 of your own — you earn an **acquisition fee** (1–3%), an
   asset-management fee, and the **promote/carry**: ~**30% of profits above an ~8% preferred return**
   to LPs (the rest split ~70/30). Other people's money, other people's risk. Overlay:
   **YOU CONTROL ~$40M · NONE OF IT YOURS**.
5. **The Developer** — you no longer buy buildings; you **build** them. Entitlements, zoning,
   construction loans, ground-up towers. Control ~**$250M** in projects. You reshape a skyline — and
   displace a neighborhood (gentrification). Overlay: **YOU CONTROL ~$250M · GROUND-UP**.
6. **The Fund / REIT** — institutional capital. A real-estate private-equity fund or a REIT, ~**$2B**
   AUM, run on a 2-and-20-style fee + promote. Homes become **yield**, a cell in a model, an
   "asset class." You never see a tenant. Overlay: **YOU CONTROL ~$2B · HOMES AS YIELD**.
7. **The Empire / Global Landlord** — Blackstone-scale: **~$611B** of real estate (Blackstone RE AUM,
   Jun 2024), the **largest commercial landlord in history**, ~**350,000** US rental homes, 12,500+
   assets across continents. Round to ~$600B on screen. Overlay: **YOU CONTROL ~$600B · 350,000 HOMES**.
8. **The People Above / The Asset Class Itself** — the capital that funds it all (pension funds,
   sovereign wealth, the central bank whose interest-rate decision **reprices every building on
   earth** overnight) and the land itself: **~$393.3 trillion** — all the real estate on the planet,
   the **largest store of wealth in human history**, ~4× global GDP (Savills, end-2024; $379.7T in
   2022, residential ~76%). They have never seen your home. They decide what it is worth.
   Overlay: **$393,000,000,000,000 · ALL REAL ESTATE ON EARTH**.

## Emotional arc (the hardening, real-estate flavor)
earnest agent saying "welcome home" → landlord signing the first eviction → investor who only sees
doors and NOI → syndicator gambling strangers' retirement money → developer bulldozing a block he
calls "blighted" → fund manager who prices shelter as yield and feels nothing → the empire that owns
350,000 front doors and knows no one behind them → the capital that decides what every home is worth
and has never stood in one.
