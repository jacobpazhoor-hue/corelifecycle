# Research — POV: You're the World's First Trillionaire  (format: scenario)

Near-future dramatized POV. There is **no trillionaire yet** (as of 2026). This is grounded in real
wealth mechanics and real numbers; the "$1 trillion net worth" is a projected milestone, flagged.
The drama is the escalation of the NUMBER and the discovery that a trillion-dollar fortune is not
money you can spend — it's a stock position that only exists if you never touch it. Money spine goes
$0 → $1M → $100M → $1B → $100B → $1T → (the number owns you) → (the appetite above money).

## The scenario ladder (level labels = escalating MONEY, not job ranks)
- **LEVEL 01 · $0** — the ordinary self: a declined card, a concrete lack, the named want.
- **LEVEL 02 · $1 MILLION** — the idea works; first real money; the co-founder.
- **LEVEL 03 · $100 MILLION** — scale; you cross from "rich" into "an institution."
- **LEVEL 04 · $1 BILLION** — you become a billionaire; the midpoint cost is paid HERE.
- **LEVEL 05 · $100 BILLION** — you move markets; you can no longer tell who is real.
- **LEVEL 06 · $1 TRILLION** — the world's first; the cold-open payoff.
- **LEVEL 07 · YOU CAN'T SELL IT** — the number owns you; you are collateral, not an owner.
- **LEVEL 08 · THE APPETITE** — what sits above money; the loop close.

## Real numbers / mechanics (the on-screen spine + share beats)

### Scale of the numbers (VERIFIED — the share-worthy "wait, that's real?" beats)
- **A million seconds is ~12 days. A billion seconds is ~31.7 years. A trillion seconds is ~31,700
  years** (≈31,688). This single comparison is the whole point: a billion and a trillion sound alike
  and are not — a trillion is ~1,000 billions. VERIFIED.
- **Spend $1,000,000 every single day since the birth of Christ and today you'd have spent ≈$740
  billion — still not one trillion.** (2026 yrs × 365.25 × $1M ≈ $740B < $1T.) VERIFIED (PolitiFact/
  Daily Signal cite ≈$733B as of 2009; grows ~$365M/yr). Use as the trillion-reveal share beat.
- **$1 trillion in $100 bills weighs ≈10,000 metric tons** (10 billion notes × ~1g). VERIFIED by
  arithmetic; commonly cited ~10,000 tonnes / would fill multiple shipping containers.

### The wealth is PAPER, not cash (the core rot — VERIFIED mechanics)
- A mega-fortune is almost entirely **unrealized equity** — the founder's stock in one company. It is
  a *number*, not a bank balance. **Selling a large stake pushes the price down** (market impact), so
  the paper worth cannot actually be converted at face value. You own a number that only stays real if
  you never touch it.
- **"Buy, borrow, die":** instead of selling (a taxable event), the ultra-wealthy **borrow against**
  their shares — banks lend billions against stock as collateral at low rates, so they get spendable
  cash and **realize no taxable income**. Real, well-documented US tax mechanic. Ground the L4/L5 beats.
- Net worth swings are enormous and unrealized: **Musk's fortune grew ~$550B in a year ≈ >$1M per
  minute** (Oxfam, 2025). The five richest men's combined wealth went **$405B → $869B (2020→2024)**,
  ≈$14M/hour, while ~5 billion people were made poorer (Oxfam "decade of division"). VERIFIED — strong
  concentration/inequality share beat.

### Where a trillion sits (VERIFIED, approximate — FLAG as "about")
- **$1T would exceed the annual GDP of all but ~15–18 countries** (comparable to the Netherlands,
  Saudi Arabia, or Switzerland; larger than the entire GDP of Sweden, Argentina, or Norway). One
  person worth more than a nation. Approximate — FLAG.
- **First trillionaire projected in the early-to-mid 2030s** (Oxfam Jan 2024: "within a decade";
  later reporting: at least five trillionaires within a decade; some tie Musk's crossing to a possible
  SpaceX IPO). VERIFIED as a *projection*, not fact — the video is explicitly near-future.
- Richest person as of 2024–2026: on the order of **$400B+** (Musk peaked ~$400–450B late 2024). No
  one has reached $1T. FLAG "as of the mid-2020s."

### Texture / jargon (woven into POV, never listed)
- **Cap table, dilution, liquidation preference, down round** (startup equity mechanics).
- **Margin loan / pledged shares / line of credit against stock** (buy-borrow-die).
- **Market cap vs personal net worth** — you can be worth more than your company earns; the number is
  a claim on the future, priced by strangers every second the market is open.
- **A trillionaire's net worth is set by other people** — the share price is a vote by millions of
  traders; you don't control it, you're exposed to it. If confidence dies, the number dies overnight.

## Story spine (see content.py PROMISE→PAYOFF LEDGER)
- **Sensory anchor:** a **19-second saved voicemail from your sister Dani** (her laugh, "call me back")
  — the one recording you never delete, replayed at every level; the cold open teases her calling LIVE
  at the trillion moment and you can't answer; the close is you replaying the old 19 seconds because
  it's all you'll ever have. Secondary sensory texture: burnt gas-station coffee (the one thing money
  never upgrades about you).
- **Named people:** Dani (sister, the reason + the emotional cost); **Sam** (co-founder/friend, the
  midpoint betrayal — you gut what she built to cross into billionaire); **Halvorsen** (the first
  investor/mentor whose warning frames the whole arc, and who tells you to burn Sam); **Rourke** (a
  rival billionaire, the midpoint taunt); **the fixer — "Keele," a private banker/family-office head**
  (shared-universe fixer; gets a dialogue line; left UNRESOLVED — he already manages whoever's next).
- **Through-line / cost line:** "You want the number. You don't know yet that the number wants you
  back." Payoff: at $1T you learn you can't spend it, can't sell it, can't quit it — you are the
  collateral, not the owner. The richest person alive owns, spendably, almost nothing.
- **Midpoint reversal (~50%):** to cross from $100M into $1B you take Halvorsen's deal, which guts
  Sam's half of the company and lays off the town that built it; Sam is gone; you didn't buy your way
  out of becoming the thing you feared — you paid to become it.
- **Loop close:** the trillion crosses, the world applauds, Dani's live call dies unanswered; a new
  kid somewhere has an idea and a declined card and a sister he's trying to save; the appetite that
  prices you never sleeps and never learns your name.
