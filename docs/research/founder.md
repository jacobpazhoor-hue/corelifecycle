# Research — Every Level of a Founder

The on-screen spine is the COMPANY VALUATION you command at each stage — from an idea worth $0 to
the venture-capital machine that decides which companies get to exist at all. The founder ladder is
not a chain of titles inside one company; it is a chain of FUNDING STAGES, each one diluting you and
enlarging the thing above you. All figures are US, 2024–2025 data. Verified figures cited; FLAGGED
items are estimates / directional.

## The real ladder (the spine = company valuation; your personal money in the sub-caption)

| # | Level | Stage | On-screen big number | Your money / stake |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Idea | nights & weekends, the deck | **$0** | no salary, savings burning |
| 2 | The Bootstrapper | pre-seed / first outside check | **$6,000,000** (post-money) | still $0 salary |
| 3 | The Seed Founder | seed round, first hires, PMF hunt | **$15,000,000** | ~$132K salary, ~$3M raised |
| 4 | The Series A | product-market fit, build the machine | **$78,000,000** | ~$179K salary, you own ~36% |
| 5 | The Scale-up | Series B/C, growth at all costs | **$400,000,000** | ~$227K salary, you own ~23% |
| 6 | The Unicorn | $1B valuation, the narrative | **$1,000,000,000** | ~$150M paper stake (~15%) |
| 7 | The Exit / IPO | ring the bell, become liquid | **$6,000,000,000** (mkt cap) | ~8–10% → a billionaire |
| 8 | The Architect | the capital above founders (VC) | **$209,000,000,000 / yr** | you decide who exists |

## Verified numbers (web, 2024–2025)

- **Seed:** median seed valuation **$14.8M** in 2024 (Carta); median seed round size ~**$2.5–3.8M**
  (rose to $3.8M by Q3 2024). On-screen seed valuation **$15M**, **$3M raised**. ✅
- **Pre-seed:** valuations cluster around **$5–6M** post-money on roughly **$0.5–1M** raised (Carta
  State of Pre-Seed). On-screen **$6M**. ✅ (round size directional ⚠️)
- **Series A:** median **post-money valuation $78.7M** in Q4 2024 (Carta), up 37% YoY; median
  pre-money ~$45M. On-screen **$78M**. ✅
- **Founder/CEO salary by stage (Kruze 2024):** Seed CEO avg **$132K**; Series A **$179K**;
  Series B **$227K**. (2025/26 drifted up: seed $147–153K, A $203K.) On-screen uses **2024**:
  $132K / $179K / $227K. ✅
- **Founder dilution / ownership (Carta, ~2,000 US software startups):** median dilution per round
  ≈ Seed 19.5%, Series A 18%, Series B 14%, Series C 10%. Founder retains **~36% after Series A**,
  **~23% after Series B**; at IPO the median founder-CEO owns roughly **8–10%**. On-screen: 36% at A,
  ~23% at B, ~15%/~$150M paper at unicorn, 8–10% at IPO. ✅
- **US venture capital deployed 2024:** **~$209 billion** (PitchBook–NVCA Venture Monitor; 3rd-highest
  of the decade, below 2021's ~$355B peak and 2022's ~$238B). **Dry powder** hit a record **~$311.6B**
  (end of 2023, ~26% of VC AUM). Global VC ~**$314B** in 2024. On-screen apex: **$209B/yr** + dry
  powder color. ✅ (exact 2024 US total cited variously $200–215B ⚠️)
- **"Unicorn"** — a private company valued ≥ **$1B**; term coined by **Aileen Lee** (Cowboy
  Ventures) in **2013** when they were rare. ✅

## FLAGGED (estimates / directional)

- **Unicorn paper stake ~$150M:** a founder owning ~15% of a $1B company = $150M ON PAPER, illiquid,
  preference-stacked. Directional; real stake varies wildly. ⚠️
- **IPO market cap $6B / "billionaire":** illustrative of a strong venture-backed IPO; many exits are
  far smaller (or acqui-hires for cents). Used as the archetypal "win." ⚠️
- **Startup failure rate:** commonly cited **~90% of startups fail**; **~75% of VC-backed startups
  never return cash** to investors (Shikhar Ghosh, HBS). Directional, widely repeated. ⚠️
- **Pre-seed round size $0.5–1M** and the pace of each level vary enormously by sector/geography. ⚠️

## Real mechanics / jargon to ground the script

- **Cap table** — the spreadsheet of who owns what; the founder's true scoreboard.
- **Dilution** — every round you sell a slice; 100% → single digits over four rounds.
- **Vesting / 4-year vest, 1-year cliff** — founders and employees earn equity over time; leave early,
  keep nothing. You can be fired from the company you started and still vest on a schedule.
- **SAFE / convertible note** — Y Combinator's instrument; raise now, price the equity later.
- **Pre-money / post-money valuation** — what you're worth before vs. after the new money lands.
- **Term sheet** — the non-binding offer that decides the board, the preferences, the control.
- **Liquidation preference (1x, participating)** — investors get paid back FIRST in a sale; founders
  and employees eat whatever's left. The clause that makes a "$200M exit" pay the founder nothing.
- **Option pool** — equity carved out for employees, almost always from the FOUNDERS' share.
- **Board seats** — by Series B the founder often no longer controls the board they answer to.
- **Runway / burn rate / default alive vs default dead** (Paul Graham) — months of cash left;
  whether you'd survive without raising again.
- **Product-market fit** (Marc Andreessen) — the only thing that matters at seed; "the market pulling
  product out of you."
- **Blitzscaling** (Reid Hoffman) / **growth at all costs** — prioritize speed over efficiency; burn
  capital to win the market. The ideology of the scale-up level.
- **Down round / bridge / the Series A crunch** — when the music stops.
- **S-1 / lock-up (180 days) / secondary sale** — the IPO paperwork, the period founders can't sell,
  the private sale of shares before exit.
- **GP / LP / carry (2-and-20) / dry powder** — the venture-fund machine: general partners run the
  fund, limited partners supply the money, carry is the 20% cut of profits. This is Level 8.

## Moral-erosion arc (the spine under the money)

earnest builder (you lived the problem) → the hustler (give away pieces, $0 salary, sleep on the
office floor) → the storyteller (you learn to sell a NARRATIVE to investors; the pitch matters more
than the truth) → growth at all costs (burn, vanity metrics, the first layoff) → the unicorn myth
(you raise on a story you no longer fully believe; the mission becomes marketing) → the exit (you
cash out; the thing you built becomes a line item; the loyal early employees' options sit underwater)
→ the capital (you stop building entirely; you ALLOCATE; you fund the pattern, not the person; you
become the venture machine that diluted you). Builder → hustler → storyteller → operator → mythmaker
→ seller → the capital that decides which founders are allowed to exist.

## The loop (open + payoff)

Open on the apex — $209B of venture capital, the people who decide which ideas get built, who were
once exactly where you are: alone, at 2am, in front of a glowing laptop. End on a NEW founder opening
a laptop in a dark bedroom at 2am with an idea and a deck — and the capital, somewhere far above,
already deciding whether she gets to exist. The machine doesn't build anything; it finds new people
who will.
