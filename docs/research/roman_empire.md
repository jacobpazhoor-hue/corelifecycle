# Your Life as Every Rank in the Roman Empire — Research (legion + cursus to the throne)

POV episode. The viewer climbs the Roman power ladder from a starving recruit (tiro) to Caesar and
then to the people *above* Caesar — the Praetorians, the legions, the Senate, the mob, the gods.
Frame as a dramatized cautionary STORY, not a documentary. Period anchor: the **early/high Empire
(Principate)**, roughly Augustus → the Severans, so pay/census numbers are internally consistent.

The drama is a **dual spine**: a real MONEY escalation (a legionary's wage → a knight's census → a
senator's census → the imperial treasury) running alongside a REACH escalation (80 men → a legion →
provinces → all of Rome). The apex twist: even the throne has a PRICE — the Guard literally
**auctioned the Empire** in 193 AD. The theme: you climb to be safe and reach the least safe seat
on earth.

## The real ladder (legion ranks + the cursus, bottom to top)
1. **Tiro (recruit)** — enrolls, swears the **sacramentum** (military oath) to the emperor; ~25-year
   term of service under the Augustan settlement. Owns nothing; trained brutally (forced marches
   ~20 Roman miles under full load, building/striking a marching camp daily).
2. **Legionary / Miles gregarius** — the ranker in the shield line. Legion ≈ 5,000 men = 10 cohorts =
   ~59–60 centuries (the first cohort had 5 double-strength centuries); the **contubernium** (8 men)
   shared a tent. Standard pay under Augustus: **225 denarii/yr** (= 900 sesterces).
3. **Principalis — Optio / Tesserarius / Signifer** — junior officers. The **optio** is the
   centurion's deputy, stands at the rear of the century to stop it breaking; paid **double**
   (duplicarius). The **signifer** (standard-bearer) also ran the unit's pay/savings bank.
4. **Centurion** — commands a **century (~80 men)**; carries the **vitis** (vine staff), his badge of
   office and his licence to flog. ~59–60 per legion, in a strict internal seniority. Pay an order of
   magnitude above a ranker (commonly cited ~15× an ordinary legionary).
5. **Primus pilus ("first spear")** — the **chief centurion** of the legion, commander of the first
   century of the first cohort; the eagle stands with him. Reaching it conferred **equestrian** status
   — the **400,000-sesterce** property qualification of a *knight*. May rise to **praefectus
   castrorum** (camp prefect, 3rd in legion command). Above the centurionate sit the 6 **tribunes**
   (one senatorial *laticlavius*, five equestrian *angusticlavii*).
6. **Legatus legionis → Legatus Augusti / General** — the **legate** (a senator with the *broad
   stripe*) commands the legion; a provincial **legatus Augusti pro praetore** commands several
   legions. A victorious general is acclaimed **IMPERATOR** ("commander") by his troops and may be
   voted a **triumph**. Senatorial census: **1,000,000 sesterces**.
7. **Emperor (Princeps / Augustus / Caesar)** — holds *imperium* over every legion, province, and
   grain ship; commander-in-chief; controls the treasury (*aerarium* / imperial *fiscus*).
8. **The people ABOVE the throne** — the **Praetorian Guard** (the only troops inside Rome; emperor-
   makers and -killers), the **frontier legions** (who proclaimed their own Caesars, e.g. the Year of
   the Four Emperors, 69 AD), the **Senate**, the **mob/grain dole**, and the **gods** (deification —
   the Senate could vote a dead emperor a god). They decide what a life, and the throne, is worth.

## The on-screen money/reach spine (gold overlay)
- Cold open / close apex: **ALL OF ROME** / **THE TRIUMPH** (the peak, foreshadowing the fall).
- L1 Tiro: **0 DENARII** — a debt and one tunic.
- L2 Legionary: **225 DENARII / YR** (Augustan standard) — *minus* deductions for food, boots, kit,
  and a burial-club levy.
- L3 Optio: **~450 DENARII / YR** — double pay (duplicarius).
- L4 Centurion: **~3,375 DENARII / YR** — ~15× a legionary; commands 80 men.
- L5 Primus pilus: **400,000 SESTERCES** — the knight's (equestrian) census; nobility by property.
- L6 Legate / Imperator: **1,000,000 SESTERCES** — the senator's census; plus a share of all plunder.
- L7 Emperor: **ALL OF ROME** — the treasury; but must pay the Guard a **donativum** to keep the seat.
- L8 Above: **25,000 SESTERCES / MAN** — the per-soldier price the Guard sold the Empire for in 193 AD.

## Real mechanics / jargon (woven into POV, never how-to)
- **sacramentum** (oath), **tiro**, **miles gregarius**, **contubernium**, **century / cohort /
  legion**, **vitis** (vine staff), **optio / signifer / aquilifer (eagle-bearer)**, **primus pilus**,
  **praefectus castrorum**, **legatus**, the **broad stripe (laticlavius)**, **imperator**, **triumph**,
  **donativum** (accession cash gift), **cursus honorum**, the equestrian (400k) and senatorial (1M)
  property censuses, **deification**.
- **The triumph:** general's face painted red (minium/vermilion) in imitation of Jupiter, riding a
  4-horse chariot through Rome; a slave held a crown over his head — and, by tradition, whispered a
  reminder of his mortality. (UNVERIFIED in exact wording — see flags.)
- **Decimation:** punishment for cowardice/mutiny — 1 man in 10 chosen by lot and beaten to death by
  the nine who stood beside him. Real, but **rare** by the Imperial period.
- **Discharge bonus (praemia):** under Augustus, ~3,000 denarii (12,000 sesterces) cash *or* a land
  grant; the *aerarium militare* (military treasury) was founded in 6 AD to fund it.

## Story spine (for the writer)
- **Mentor:** **Drusus** — a grizzled centurion with the vine staff who trains you, names his dead,
  and teaches the brutal truth ("most men don't get the land"). He falls in battle; you **break his
  fingers to take the vitis** — your promotion-into-centurion payoff. His dying word "**Hold them**"
  becomes a recurring line that pays off at your assassination.
- **The home you climb for (named person):** your sister **Livia**, back in the village, eating barley
  paste; you joined to win **land** and buy back the seized family farm and go home. The cost: the
  higher you rise, the further from home — at the top you own the world and have nowhere to return.
- **Theme line (woven):** *"You climb to be safe; the throne is the most dangerous chair ever built."*
  Anaphora at the L5 peak: *"Money buys silver. Money buys rank. Money cannot buy the road home."*
- **Open loop (cold open):** the **triumph** — gold chariot, red paint, the slave's whisper "remember
  you are mortal." Resolved at L6 (you live the triumph) and paid off at L8 (the whisper returns as
  you are murdered). **Cyclical close:** a new boy of seventeen swears the same oath; the chariot is
  always waiting, it only changes who stands in it.
- **Share-worthy "wait — that's real?" beats:** the 193 AD **auction of the Empire**; **decimation**;
  the **triumph whisper**; **Vespasian's deathbed joke** ("I think I am becoming a god").

## FLAGS / unverified
- Legionary **225 denarii/yr** under Augustus — **verified** (standard figure; raised to 300 by
  Domitian in 84 AD — the episode stays in the Augustan frame).
- **Optio = double pay (~450)** — optio was a *duplicarius*; **broadly verified**, exact step not fixed.
- **Centurion ~15× / ~3,375 denarii** — **APPROXIMATE** (estimates range ~15–17×; absolute figures
  debated; Domitian-era ordinary centurion sometimes cited ~13,500 denarii). Flagged as a round figure.
- **Primus pilus → equestrian 400,000 sesterces** — **verified** (primipilate conferred equestrian
  rank/qualification); equestrian census 400k and senatorial census 1M sesterces — **verified**.
- **Discharge praemia ~3,000 denarii or land; aerarium militare 6 AD** — **verified**.
- **Donativum: Claudius paid the Praetorians 15,000 sesterces/man (41 AD)** — **verified** (Suetonius;
  often cited as the first emperor to openly buy the Guard). Praetorian strength ~**9,000** — approximate.
- **193 AD auction of the Empire; Didius Julianus, 25,000 sesterces/man, reigned ~9 weeks** —
  **verified** (Cassius Dio, Herodian); per-man figure ~25,000 sesterces.
- **Triumph: red-painted face + slave whispering a mortality reminder** — the red paint and crown are
  **verified**; the *slave-whisper* and its exact words ("respice post te; hominem te memento" /
  "memento mori") come from **Tertullian** and are **traditionally reported / UNVERIFIED** as universal practice.
- **Decimation** — **verified** as a punishment but **rare** in the Imperial era; dramatized here.
- **Caligula killed by the Praetorians (Cassius Chaerea, 41 AD); Claudius found behind a curtain** —
  **verified** (Suetonius/Josephus).
- **Vespasian: "Vae, puto deus fio" ("Dear me, I think I am becoming a god")** — **verified** (Suetonius).
- **"More emperors murdered than died in bed"** — **APPROXIMATE**: a large share of Principate emperors
  died violently (assassination/suicide/in battle); use as "more died by the knife than in bed," flagged.
- Legion ≈ **5,000 men / 10 cohorts / ~59–60 centuries**, **~25-year** service, **contubernium = 8** —
  **verified** (standard high-Empire structure).
