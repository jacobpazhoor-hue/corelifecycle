# Your Life in the Mafia at Every Rank — Research (American Cosa Nostra ladder)

POV episode. The viewer climbs the ranks of an American Mafia "family" (La Cosa Nostra) — from a
neighborhood kid running errands to the don at the head of the table, and then to the people ABOVE the
don: the Commission, and above even that, the two forces that end every mob life — RICO and the rat.
Frame as a **dramatized cautionary STORY**, never how-to. The drama is a **rising money/earn spine**
(a few hundred a week → a $500M-a-year family → the mob tax on a whole city) shadowed by a sub-caption
that keeps the truth: none of it is yours, you kick up forever, and there are only two ways out — a box
or a cell.

## The real ladder (La Cosa Nostra — a New York "family")
1. **Associate** — NOT a member. Anyone (need not be Italian) who works with the family: runs errands,
   books, numbers, moves swag, earns. Does the dirty work, gets a cut, has no vote and no protection.
2. **Soldier (soldato / "made man" / "button")** — a formally **inducted** member. You are "made"
   only after you're sponsored and (traditionally) you've "**made your bones**" — committed a killing.
   Sworn in on the **omertà** oath (a saint's card burned in your cupped hands: *"if I betray this, may
   I burn like this saint"*). Runs rackets, **kicks up** a share every week to his capo. Now untouchable
   — you can't be hit without the Commission's approval — but you belong to the family for life.
3. **Caporegime (capo / captain)** — commands a **crew** of soldiers (a handful to ~20+) and their
   associates; controls a territory and its rackets; passes tribute up to the boss.
4. **Consigliere** — the **counselor / advisor** to the boss; mediates disputes, no crew of his own,
   a voice not a sword. (Often shown parallel to the underboss.)
5. **Underboss (sottocapo)** — **second in command**; runs day-to-day, relays the boss's orders,
   heir apparent. (Sammy "the Bull" Gravano was Gambino underboss — and the man who flipped.)
6. **Boss (don / capo)** — head of the family. Takes a **cut of everything** every earner brings in;
   his word is law; approves the hits. Also the biggest target — for rivals and for the Feds.
7. **The Commission** — the **national board of directors** of the Mafia. Formed **1931 by Charles
   "Lucky" Luciano** after the Castellammarese War, replacing the single *capo di tutti i capi* ("boss
   of all bosses") with a ruling committee: the bosses of New York's **Five Families** (Gambino,
   Genovese, Lucchese, Colombo, Bonanno) plus the **Chicago Outfit** and, at times, others. It
   allocates territory, settles wars, and must approve the killing of a made man or a boss.
8. **ABOVE the mob** — the forces no don outranks:
   - **RICO** (Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act, **1970**) — lets prosecutors charge
     the boss for crimes his soldiers committed, as one "criminal enterprise." The **Commission Trial
     (1985–86, US v. Salerno)** used it to convict eight men; **most got 100-year sentences** (Jan 13,
     1987): Salerno, Persico, Corallo, Langella. It broke the Commission.
   - **The rat** — omertà, sworn on fire, ends at a plea deal. **Joe Valachi** (1963) first said the
     name "Cosa Nostra" out loud to Congress. **Sammy Gravano** confessed to **19 murders** and served
     about **five years** by testifying against John Gotti (9 days on the stand) — Gotti, the "Teflon
     Don," was convicted of five murders and died in prison.
   - **The government / the legend** — the money is seized, the name becomes a movie. You end up dead
     or in a cell; the winners are the lawyers, the informant, and the Feds who take it all.

## The on-screen spine (THE EARN — money, rising, with the cost in the sub-caption)
Big gold number = what flows through you (the earn/the family's gross); the SUB keeps the truth
(you kick up, none is yours, the body count, the two ways out). Illustrative figures — real mob income
was cash and never audited; flagged where estimated.
- **L0 cold-open (apex):** the family at the top — **$500,000,000 / YR** flowing to the table (est.,
  Gambino-scale under Gotti) — and a wire under the table.
- **L1 Associate:** **~$200 / WK.** (you keep half; the rest kicks up.) *[illustrative]*
- **L2 Soldier (made):** **~$250K / YR** off rackets. Sub: MADE · you kick up every week. *[illustrative]*
- **L3 Capo:** **~$1M / YR** — a crew of earners, a territory. *[illustrative]*
- **L4 Underboss:** **~$5M / YR** — second in command. *[illustrative]*
- **L5 Boss:** **$500M / YR** — the family's gross; your personal cut in millions, and a target on you.
- **L6 Commission:** **A WHOLE CITY** — the mob tax: **2% of every NYC concrete contract $2M–$15M**
  kicked to the Commission (the "Concrete Club"); the Javits Center concrete alone ran ~$12M over free
  market. Plus the Vegas **skim**: the Stardust bled **~$400,000 a MONTH in untaxed cash** to the mob.
- **L7–L8:** the number collapses — **SEIZED / $0** — RICO takes it, the rat walks, you get **THE BOX
  OR THE CELL.**

## Real mechanics / jargon (woven into POV, never how-to)
- **This thing of ours** (*cosa nostra*), **omertà** (code of silence), **made / getting made / the
  button**, **making your bones** (first kill), **kick up / tribute**, **the crew**, **a sit-down**
  (dispute meeting), **going to the mattresses** (war footing), **the skim**, **the shylock / vig**
  (loansharking interest), **the numbers** (illegal lottery), **on the record / off the record**,
  **the Commission**, **capo di tutti i capi**, **RICO**, **the rat / flipping / WITSEC**.
- Real rackets: gambling (numbers), loansharking, labor/union racketeering, extortion, the docks
  (longshoremen), garbage carting, the garment district, construction (the Concrete Club), narcotics
  (the "Pizza Connection," $1.6B in heroin, 1985 trial), Vegas casino skim.

## Story spine (for the writer)
- **Mentor:** **Sal** — a capo who spots you on the corner, vouches for you, teaches the rules and the
  oath, treats you like a son. The betrayal payoff: the Commission marks Sal, and rising means you must
  set him up (or die beside him) — the **midpoint reversal** (~50%): the thing you wanted starts to rot.
- **The rat (recurring):** **Nicky** — your protégé, the soldier you bring up, your underboss. He is the
  open loop from the cold open. At the top, **Nicky wears the wire** and takes the stand — omertà dies in
  your own family, the way it really died (Gravano→Gotti). Loop payoff of the cold-open dinner.
- **Home/want:** a poor immigrant block; your mother works two jobs; the neighborhood don glides by in a
  Cadillac and the whole street bows. Concrete lack: **eleven dollars in a coffee can**, macaroni and
  cabbage. Named want: the Cadillac, the respect, to never be scared on your own corner again.
- **Theme (anaphora):** *"This thing of ours buys respect. This thing of ours buys fear. This thing of
  ours can't buy your way out."* / *"There are only two ways out of this life — a box or a cell."*
- **Cold open loop:** the boss's table, everyone standing when you enter — and the wire you don't know
  about. Pays off at the RICO trial + Nicky on the stand. **Cyclical close:** a new kid on the same
  corner, first errand, dreaming of the Cadillac — the corner only changes who stands on it.
- **Share-worthy "that's real?" beats:** the 2% mob tax on every skyscraper's concrete; the Stardust
  bleeding $400K a month in *untaxed* cash; you had to kill to get "made"; Gravano — 19 murders, ~5
  years; the bosses got **100-year** sentences; the Commission was literally a *board of directors*
  founded in 1931.

## FLAGS / unverified
- Commission founded 1931 by Luciano; Five Families + Chicago; board-of-directors role — **verified**
  (Wikipedia: The Commission; History.com).
- Commission Trial 1985–86, RICO, US v. Salerno, 8 convicted, most 100-year sentences 1987 — **verified**
  (Wikipedia: Mafia Commission Trial; TIME).
- Concrete Club: contracts $2M–$15M reserved for the club, **2% kickback** to the Commission; Javits
  ~$12M overcharge — **verified** (City Journal; corfix; NationalCrimeSyndicate).
- Vegas skim: Stardust ~**$400K/month** untaxed; ~$7–15M via rigged scales 1974–76; Strawman indictment
  ~$1.6M+ — **verified** (Mob Museum; NationalCrimeSyndicate).
- Gravano: **19 murders, 5 years**, underboss, flipped on Gotti (9 days), Gotti convicted of 5 murders —
  **verified** (Wikipedia; ABC News).
- **Personal/family income figures ($200/wk → $500M/yr) are ILLUSTRATIVE** dramatizations — real mob
  income was untaxed cash and never audited; the $500M Gambino-gross figure is a commonly cited estimate,
  treated as "about half a billion." Ranks (associate→soldier→capo→consigliere/underboss→boss→Commission)
  are the standard, well-documented LCN structure.
