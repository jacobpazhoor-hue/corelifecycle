# Research — Hitman (contract killer) at Every Level

Format: **rank**. Title spine: *Your Life as a Hitman at Every Level (…)*. Level labels
`LEVEL 01 · THE …`. DRAMATIZED CAUTIONARY POV — never a how-to. Frame killing as a trap that
closes, not a fantasy; the apex twist subverts the "cool assassin" myth with the documented truth.

## The core real-world spine (VERIFIED — this is the grounding + the share beats)

**Birmingham City University study** — Prof. David Wilson, Dr Elizabeth Yardley, Donal MacIntyre,
Liam Brolan, *The Howard Journal of Criminal Justice* (pub. Jan 2014). Data: **27 contract killings
committed by 36 hitmen on the British mainland, 1974–2013.**
- **Average fee: £15,180.** Lowest **£200**, highest **£100,000**. (≈ $18–25k average in the story's
  gold overlay terms; I round the STORY's average to "about twenty thousand dollars.")
- **Real four-type taxonomy** → I use it verbatim as the low ladder rungs: **NOVICE → DILETTANTE →
  JOURNEYMAN → MASTER.** (Then the story ESCALATES beyond the documented record into the mythic
  tiers — the Cleaner / the Ghost / the Handler / the open contract — clearly a dramatization.)
- Average hitman age **38**; average victim age **36**; ages ranged **15 to 63** (the youngest
  contract killer in the study was **15** — a share beat).
- **Guns** were the weapon in **25 of 35** victims.
- Signature finding (the myth-buster, and my APEX share beat): the real British contract killer is
  NOT a suave untraceable professional — he is a **local man, paid about £15k, who often kills the
  victim in a suburban street (famously "while walking the dog") and frequently gets caught or
  botches it.** The Hollywood "ghost who retires to a beach" is largely fiction.
  → Source: Birmingham City University news release + ScienceDaily (2014-01-27).

**FBI (US) — murder-for-hire reality:**
- Quoted real fees have ranged from **$600 to kill a girlfriend up to $25,000 to kill a spouse.**
- The FBI works, on average, **70–90 murder-for-hire cases a year** — most are undercover stings;
  the "customer" is very often talking to an agent. (Second share beat: the person you hire is,
  statistically, disturbingly likely to be a cop or an informant.)
  → Source: FBI "Murder-for-Hire" story; Slate "How much does it cost to put a hit on someone?" (2009).

**Australia study (Mouzos & Venditto, AIC):** 163 contract murders/attempts 1989–2002; fees
**$5,000–$30,000**, **average ~$15,000.** Corroborates the ~$15k global average at street level.

**Russia:** contracts documented from a couple hundred dollars; high-profile chain hits (e.g. the
1998 killing of Duma deputy Galina Starovoitova) reportedly ~**$150,000** because of the long chain
of intermediaries. (Shows price = difficulty + prominence + chain length, not skill alone.)

## Real jargon / mechanics (woven into POV, never listed — cautionary, no operational detail)
- **The contract / the paper / the name and the number** — a job is "a name and a number."
- **Cut-out / the broker / the handler** — layers between buyer and shooter so no one can trace up
  the chain (this is exactly why the FBI stings work: they insert themselves as the cut-out).
- **Novice / dilettante / journeyman / master** — the real academic typology (Wilson).
- **"Walking the dog"** — the study's grim signature: most real hits happen in mundane suburban
  moments, not glamorous ones. (Used as a chilling beat, not instruction.)
- **Cold / no connection to the target** — the professional ideal (never kill someone you know) →
  the midpoint reversal breaks exactly this rule.
- Deliberately NO methods, no tradecraft specifics, no how-to — the POV stays on dread and cost.

## Share beats (the "wait — that's real?" plants; both VERIFIED above)
1. **The average contract killing costs about the price of a used car (~£15k / ~$20k), the killer is
   usually a local man of ~38, and he often gets caught — the untraceable ghost is mostly a myth.**
2. **The FBI runs 70–90 murder-for-hire stings a year; the "professional" a first-timer hires is,
   more often than not, an informant or an agent.** (Also: the youngest contract killer on record in
   the UK study was 15.)
