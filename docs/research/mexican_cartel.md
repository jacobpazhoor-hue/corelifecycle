# Research — Your Life in a Cartel at Every Rank (mexican_cartel)

**Format:** rank (crime ladder). **Frame:** cautionary DRAMATIZED POV — the viewer lives the climb and
learns the only two exits are the grave or the cell ("la tumba o la cárcel"). NO how-to: production,
smuggling methods, and weapons are named only as career milestones/settings, never as instructions.
This is an anti-glamour story: the money climbs, the exit closes, and you own nothing at the end.

Numbers below are cross-checked where possible; cartel finances are opaque and estimates vary WILDLY,
so ranges are flagged. On-screen the $ spine is the DRAMA (an escalation), not an accounting claim.

## The ladder (the on-screen levels)
1. **LEVEL 01 · EL HALCÓN (the lookout)** — a kid on a corner/rooftop with a cheap phone and a whistle,
   watching for police/rivals and calling it in. Entry role; often recruited as a child. Pay is a
   pittance — a few hundred pesos a week, "three times minimum wage" to a poor kid, so it feels like a
   fortune. VERIFIED: cartels recruit minors as halcones and even hitmen (VICE; multiple reports).
   Spine: **~$600 / MONTH** (feels like the world; buys your mother's medicine).
2. **LEVEL 02 · EL BURRERO (the mule / runner)** — you move product: across the line, up the corridor,
   between stash houses. Paid per load. The first time you carry something that can end you.
   Spine: **$5,000 / LOAD** (illustrative; runner pay varies from hundreds to thousands per trip).
3. **LEVEL 03 · EL SICARIO (the gunman)** — you make your bones. Enforcer for the plaza. Base pay is
   shockingly low; the myth is the money, the reality is the risk. VERIFIED ranges: base as low as
   ~$105–200/MONTH; per-hit figures reported from **as low as $85 a hit** up to a few hundred–few
   thousand; one ex-sicario described killing "for $30 a time" (Baverstock, 2015). SHAREABLE TRUE BEAT:
   a street sicario's pay is a poverty wage AND the life is measured in a handful of years — you are
   the cheapest, most disposable thing in the whole economy. Spine: **$85 / BODY** (the horror number).
4. **LEVEL 04 · JEFE DE PLAZA (the plaza boss)** — you own a corridor. You tax everyone who moves
   through it — "el piso" (the floor tax) on dealers, businesses, migrants. Millions a year flow up
   through you. You also pay UP and pay off ("plata o plomo" — silver or lead — the bribe or the
   bullet). Spine: **$40M / YEAR** (the plaza's flow; little of it is yours to keep).
5. **LEVEL 05 · EL LUGARTENIENTE (the lieutenant / underboss)** — you run sicarios and plazas, wage the
   war with rival cartels and the army. The disappearances, the mass graves, the pressure. Spine:
   **$300M** and a body count that stops being people.
6. **LEVEL 06 · EL PATRÓN (the kingpin)** — you run the cartel. A walled compound in the sierra, a
   tunnel under the bedroom, private zoo, an army of gunmen, judges and police on payroll. Forbes once
   listed a real kingpin (Chapo Guzmán) at roughly **$1 BILLION** net worth. Cartel annual revenue
   estimates: **$3B–$39B/year** (Sinaloa; wildly contested). Spine: **$1B+** and dogs that go quiet at
   4am is the only warning you'll get.
7. **LEVEL 07 · LA RED (the network / the empire)** — extradition, the DEA/HSI wire, the market that
   spans continents (Europe, Asia). The enterprise is bigger than any one patrón; take one down and it
   barely stutters. Spine: **THE MARKET** — the thing you served.
8. **LEVEL 08 · LA DEMANDA (the demand up north)** — above the kingpin sits the buyer who never sees
   you: **~$150 BILLION/year** Americans spend on illicit drugs (RAND, 2016 — cannabis/cocaine/heroin/
   meth). The market that made you, spends you, and replaces you the day you fall. You were never the
   top. You were inventory. Spine: **$150B DEMAND**.

## The cost (the through-line: "no way out")
- **Plata o plomo** (silver or lead): the cartel's recruitment/corruption logic — take the money or take
  the bullet. Once you're in, refusal is death. There is no resignation, only prison or a grave.
- Mexico's drug war (since Dec 2006, when Calderón militarized it): death-toll estimates range from
  ~28,000 (2010) to 60,000 (2012 official) to **well over 120,000–150,000+** by later years, with
  **100,000+ disappeared/missing** (figures contested; sources: CNN, Wilson Center, Frontline/PBS,
  Wikipedia "Mexican drug war"). Use as a scale/cost beat, flagged as an estimate range.
- Sicario life is short and cheap; the disposable bottom of a $150B pyramid.
- Folk saints: narcos venerate **Jesús Malverde** ("the narco-saint," a Sinaloan folk figure) and
  **Santa Muerte** (Holy Death). Shrines and medallions are real cultural markers — used here as the
  sensory anchor (a cheap saint's medallion) and a cautionary irony, never an endorsement.

## Sensory anchor motif
A **cheap plastic saint's medallion on a bootlace at your throat** — your mother ties it on at Level 1;
you press it to your lips before every job; it's cold plastic that later becomes gold that means
nothing; at the end it's cut from your throat and tied onto a new lookout. Touch + the smell of it
(diesel, cut lime, gun oil) carry the callback.

## Jargon (woven into POV, never listed)
halcón (lookout), burrero (mule), sicario (gunman), jefe de plaza / plaza (turf + its boss), el piso
(the floor/turf tax), plata o plomo (silver or lead), el patrón / el jefe (the boss), la sierra (the
mountains), corridos (the ballads that mythologize narcos), levantón (a forced abduction — named as a
dread, not depicted). Keep Spanish light and translated in-line.
