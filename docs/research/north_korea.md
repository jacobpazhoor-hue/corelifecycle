## The ladder + escalation spine

North Korea has no real wage economy for most of this climb — the drama isn't salary, it's **who eats,
who is watched, and who is allowed to exist in which city**. Spine is a DUAL non-money ladder (pairs
with [[inverted-spine-validated]] / [[roman-dual-spine-validated]]): the **PDS grain ration** (grams/day,
collapsing to zero at the bottom under real famine conditions) for levels 1–3, pivoting to a
**privilege/access** stat (population you outrank, hard currency you touch) once you're inside the
apparatus, and landing on Office 39's real hard-currency total at the apex.

1. **The farm (songbun "wavering"/"hostile" class)** — Songbun is NK's hereditary loyalty-caste system,
   inherited from your family's WWII/Korean-War-era conduct: **core/loyal ~25%**, **wavering ~55%**,
   **hostile** the remainder — 51 original sub-categories under 3 broad classes [VERIFIED — HRW,
   Foreign Affairs, VOA]. It determines housing, job, school, and who you're allowed to marry. Official
   PDS target for a working adult was **~700g/day of grain**, but delivery collapsed for years at a
   time — the 1990s famine ("the Arduous March," 1994–1998) killed somewhere between **240,000 and
   3.5 million** by outside estimate; Pyongyang's own figure is 220,000–235,000; a commonly cited
   mid-range is **600,000–1,000,000 of a 20M population** [VERIFY — genuinely disputed, cite the range,
   never a single number as fact]. Overlay: **700G/DAY (TARGET) → RATIONS STOP.**
2. **The candidate (Korean Workers' Party recruit)** — KWP membership is by recommendation only, often
   earned through military service or "loyalty deeds"; membership is roughly **3–6.5M of ~25–26M
   people** [VERIFY — no current official figure published; 1988 figure 3M, later estimates to 6.5M].
   Real duty at this level: the **inminban** (neighborhood unit, ~20–40 households) where members are
   expected to report on each other — the informant mechanism that runs the whole system.
3. **The cadre (full party member, provincial official)** — songbun can rise (never for the hostile
   class) through "extraordinary loyalty"; better food access, tolerance to trade in the unofficial
   **jangmadang** markets that emerged after the PDS broke down. Overlay shifts to privilege: an
   average Pyongyang resident earns **~$30–40/month**; upper-tier officials **take home thousands of
   dollars a month** off books [VERIFIED — reporting cited via lovemoney/RFA-sourced roundup].
4. **Pyongyang (the capital, restricted-entry)** — only the "core/nucleus" ~10% of the population may
   legally reside in Pyongyang at all; residents there get sugar, oil, and eggs in their ration that
   the rest of the country doesn't see [VERIFIED — RFA / State Dept human-rights reporting]. The
   sensory anchor escalates here (see below).
5. **Central Committee** — the Party's ruling body; the 7th Congress (2017) seated **235 full members**
   [VERIFIED, Wikipedia/globalsecurity]. **MIDPOINT REVERSAL**: the real, documented **guilt-by-
   association policy (연좌제 / "three generations")** — a single person's political offense can send
   parents, spouse, and children to a kwanliso camp together [VERIFIED — HRW/Amnesty reporting on
   Kwanliso camps]. This is the "wait — that's real?" share beat and the story's midpoint cost.
6. **The Politburo** — the top decision body: as of the mid-2020s composed of roughly **19 full members
   + 11 alternates** [VERIFY — count fluctuates by reshuffle]. **Kwanliso political prison camps**
   currently hold an estimated **80,000–120,000 people** (wider historical estimates to 200,000);
   satellite imagery on Kwanliso 15 (Yodok) suggested ~50,000 held in 2011, Kwanliso 16 (Hwasong)
   ~20,000 — camps are real, satellite-confirmed, still active [VERIFIED — Amnesty International
   satellite analysis].
7. **The court** — the tightest inner circle around the leader (personal secretariat, bodyguard corps,
   art/culture apparatus that vets everything the leader sees); total surveillance runs both ways —
   the people closest to power are also the most watched and the most purgeable.
8. **The apex — Office 39** — the Kim family's hard-currency slush fund (est. founded 1970s under Kim
   Il-sung), running **120+ front enterprises** (mining, shipping, hotels, insurance fraud, counterfeit
   currency), estimated to bring in **$500M–$1B+/year** [VERIFIED, multiple outlets, wide range
   flagged]. Even at this apex, the story's cost line holds: no one — cadre or Politburo member or
   family retainer — is free to simply leave the country. Attempting to cross the northern border
   (Tumen/Yalu rivers into China) without state permission is defection, treated as a capital political
   crime; border guards are ordered to shoot on sight in some zones [VERIFIED — long-standing, widely
   reported NK/China border policy, not detailed as method, only as the story's cost/stakes].

## The visual/sensory anchor — VERIFIED and genuinely re-triggerable
The **leader-portrait lapel badge**: all adult citizens are required to wear one of **20+ badge
designs**, and the design worn is a real, documented status marker — a plain circular Kim Il-sung
portrait pin is common/low-status; the rarest and most prestigious badge carries **both** Kim Il-sung
and Kim Jong-il together on a red flag/banner design, reserved for **high-level Workers' Party
officials only** — seeing one in the wild reportedly startles junior bureaucrats [VERIFIED, RFA/
Wikipedia]. This is the episode's sensory anchor motif: the badge at your chest, upgraded (never
removed) at every level-up — clean and cheap and singular at Level 1, dual-portrait and gold-edged by
the Central Committee, and, at the very end, no badge visible at all because the people who guard the
Family don't need to prove anything to anyone. A DRAWABLE prop (a small red pin at the collarbone),
addressing the pack's usual undrawn-anchor gap.

## Emotional arc
hunger/hope → forced complicity (informing on a neighbor) → careful ambition → the privileged blindness
of Pyongyang → the cold calculus of the apparatus → total paranoia at the center of power → the specific
horror of being trusted completely by a system that trusts no one → realizing the cage was never the
farm, it was the border, the whole time.
