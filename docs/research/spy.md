# Your Life as a Spy at Every Level — Research (CIA / HUMINT case-officer ladder)

POV episode. The viewer is a CIA **clandestine** officer (Directorate of Operations / case-officer
track), climbing from recruit to the people who *task* the spies. Frame as dramatized cautionary
STORY, never instructional tradecraft. The drama is the **inverted spine**: personal pay stays small
and on the federal GS scale, while REACH (assets → a station → a region → the whole clandestine
service → the agency → the intelligence community) explodes. Apex flips to the **policymakers + the
$106B secret budget** that decide what is worth knowing and who pays for it.

## The real ladder (CIA Directorate of Operations — case-officer / operations-officer track)
1. **The Recruit / Career Trainee (CT)** — selected, polygraphed, cleared TS/SCI, sent to "the Farm"
   (the Agency's training site) to learn tradecraft. Knows nothing yet; runs no one.
2. **Case Officer (Operations Officer)** — first overseas tour, usually under **official cover**.
   The core job: spot, assess, develop, **recruit**, and **run** foreign **assets** (agents/sources)
   who hand over secrets. You don't steal secrets — you persuade *people* to. Runs a handful of assets.
3. **Senior Case Officer / Branch** — runs a **network** of assets across a city/target; mentors
   junior officers; handles the higher-value, higher-risk sources.
4. **Chief of Station (COS)** — runs **all** CIA operations in one country: the station's officers,
   its networks, the relationship with the host service and the U.S. ambassador. The top spy in-country.
5. **Division / Senior Intelligence Service (SIS)** — back at headquarters, **Chief of a regional
   division** (e.g., a whole part of the world): multiple stations, many countries. SIS ≈ the
   Senior Executive Service (flag rank) of the spy world.
6. **Director of the Directorate of Operations (DDO)** (formerly the National Clandestine Service) —
   runs **every** human/covert operation the Agency conducts, worldwide.
7. **Director of the CIA (DCIA)** — runs the whole Agency (~20,000+ officers; a classified, multi-
   billion slice of the national budget).
8. **The people ABOVE** — the **Director of National Intelligence (DNI)** coordinating ~18 IC
   elements; the **President / National Security Council** who *task* the intelligence and act (or
   not) on it; and the **secret budget** that funds it all. They never meet an asset. They decide
   what a secret — and a life — is worth.

## The on-screen spine (REACH, not money — the inverted spine, like the soldier ep)
Big gold number = how far your reach extends; personal GS pay rides the SUB-caption, small and capped.
- L0 apex cold-open: **$106,000,000,000** — the disclosed FY2024 U.S. intelligence budget (verified).
- L1 Recruit: ASSETS YOU RUN **0**.  Pay ~**$66K** (GS-10/11). Verified band: ~$56K–$96K entry.
- L2 Case officer: ASSETS YOU RUN **~4** (one holds a state's nuclear/leadership secret). Pay ~$78K.
- L3 Senior CO: a NETWORK — **~20 ASSETS** across a city. Pay ~$96K.
- L4 Chief of Station: **1 COUNTRY** — a station of officers + hundreds of sources. Pay ~$140K.
- L5 Division Chief (SIS): **A REGION — 12+ COUNTRIES**. Pay ~$190K (SIS/SES band; verified avg ~$202K).
- L6 Director of Operations: **EVERY COVERT OP ON EARTH**. Pay ~$200K (still federal — never rich).
- L7 DCIA: **~21,000 OFFICERS** under you; a classified budget. Pay caps near the federal ceiling.
- L8 Above: **$106,000,000,000 · 18 AGENCIES** — the DNI, the President, the customers who task it.

## Real mechanics / jargon (woven into POV, never how-to)
- **Tradecraft:** surveillance detection route (SDR), **dead drop**, **brush pass**, signal site
  (a chalk mark), **safehouse**, **cover** (official vs non-official / NOC), alias, the "wall" of
  pinned photos + map + string in a target room.
- **The asset cycle:** spot → assess → **develop** → **recruit** ("the pitch") → **run / handle** →
  and, when the mission demands, **burn** (the cost — the human you spent). **MICE** is the old
  recruiter's shorthand for why people betray: Money, Ideology, Coercion, Ego — use it as theme, not manual.
- **Cover stories, the polygraph ("the box"), compartmentation ("need to know"), the SCIF/vault,
  exfiltration.** A station, the COS, "the seventh floor" (CIA leadership), Langley/HQ.
- The clandestine service used to be the **National Clandestine Service**; it's now the
  **Directorate of Operations**. The DNI sits atop ~18 intelligence elements created after 2004.

## Story spine (for the writer)
- **Mentor:** **Mercer** — a legendary case officer "a room goes quiet for"; recruits and trains you,
  teaches you the asset is a person, then shows you the cost is too. You measure yourself against him.
- **The human cost (recurring face):** **Yusuf** — your first real asset, a foreign source who
  trusts you because *you* told him you'd protect him. The mission eventually spends him. He is the
  open loop: "you don't know yet what it costs."
- **Theme line (woven):** *"The work is people. You spend them."* / "Trust is the only weapon you
  carry, and it always goes off in someone else's hand."
- **Arc:** want to matter / see behind the curtain → recruit and run people → trade Yusuf for the
  mission → become the handler who tasks young officers to do the same → at the top, learn you were an
  asset too, run by people you'll never meet. **Loop:** end on a new recruit at the Farm / you making a
  pitch the way Mercer once pitched you — the door opens for someone new.
