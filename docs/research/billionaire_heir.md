## The real "ladder" of generational wealth (the levels)
Generational wealth has no job titles — the ladder is **structural**: how close you are to control
of the fortune, and how the fortune is legally caged so no single heir can spend it.

1. **THE HEIR (minor beneficiary)** — you own nothing; trusts own everything around you. Your money
   is an allowance. Custodial accounts (UGMA/UTMA) at most. The estate, the art, the jet: titled to
   trusts and LLCs, not people.
2. **THE STUDENT** — elite boarding schools as network-formation. Institut Le Rosey (Switzerland) is
   the world's most expensive school: fees ≈ CHF 115,500–130,000/yr (~$130K) — VERIFIED (Guinness /
   Spear's). Kings, shahs, and billionaires' children; the point is the address book, not the classes.
3. **THE TRUST FUND (25/30/35)** — classic staggered distributions at ages 25/30/35; a **spendthrift
   trust** with trustee discretion under the **HEMS** standard (health, education, maintenance,
   support). A monthly draw (five figures is typical at the $1B tier) before the first distribution.
   NDAs for staff and romantic partners are standard UHNW practice.
4. **THE FAMILY OFFICE APPRENTICE** — the machine that runs the money. ~**8,000 single family
   offices** globally (2024), projected ~10,720 by 2030; AUM ~$3.1T → $5.4T by 2030 — VERIFIED
   (Deloitte). Informal entry ticket ≈ **$100M+**; running one costs ~1–2% of assets/yr.
   Core mechanics the apprentice learns:
   - **Buy, Borrow, Die** (term: Prof. Ed McCaffery, USC): never sell appreciated assets — borrow
     against them at low single digits (SBLOCs), realize no capital gains, then die: **step-up in
     basis** (IRC §1014) erases the embedded gains for heirs. Estate tax is 40% on paper
     (exemption ~$15M/person from 2026) — but structured families pay near zero across transfers.
   - **GRATs** (grantor-retained annuity trusts) — the "Walton GRAT," named for Audrey Walton's 1993
     case; used by Waltons, Bloomberg, Zuckerberg-tier fortunes to pass appreciation tax-free.
   - 100s of LLCs; the family bank; art in freeports; foundation as the public face.
5. **THE SUCCESSOR** — succession is governed by a **family constitution** and a **family council**
   vote, not primogeniture; trust-protector and successor-trustee designations decide who holds the
   keys. Estate/trust contests between heirs are common and burn years + eight-figure legal fees.
6. **THE STEWARD (patriarch/matriarch)** — runs the machine. The **private foundation** must pay out
   only **5%/yr** (IRS minimum-distribution rule) — the rest compounds forever; philanthropy doubles
   as reputation and access (galas, senators, naming rights).
7. **THE DYNASTY ARCHITECT** — perpetual **dynasty trusts**. South Dakota: perpetual trusts, no state
   income tax, **>$500B** now sitting in SD trust companies (100+ of them, doubled in ~5 years) —
   VERIFIED (multiple; The Nation/tax sources). Nevada allows **365-year** trusts. GST-exempt dynasty
   trusts compound outside the estate-tax system for generations. Old money's oldest (apocryphal,
   attributed-to-Rockefeller) rule: **"own nothing, control everything."** The Rockefeller family
   office ("Room 5600", 30 Rockefeller Plaza) has kept ~7 generations and 170+ heirs funded since
   the 1880s.
8. **THE FAMILIES NO LIST NAMES** — the apex is invisible. Cargill-MacMillan: **21 billionaires in
   one family** (most of any family on Earth), owners of America's largest private company
   (~$154–160B revenue) — VERIFIED (Forbes/Wikipedia). Mars, Koch. Many decline or obstruct rich-list
   valuation entirely. The Waltons (richest family, ~$267B on the 2024 Forbes list) gained ~$173B in
   a single year on Walmart's 2024 run — ≈ **$20M an hour** — FLAGGED: derived arithmetic, order-of-
   magnitude honest.

## The two numbers that frame the whole episode (both verified)
- **$124 TRILLION** — the Great Wealth Transfer through 2048 (Cerulli): $105T to heirs, $18T to
  charity; >half of it ($62T) from HNW/UHNW households = just **2% of families**.
- **70% / 90%** — the Williams Group 20-year study of 3,200 wealthy families: 70% of fortunes are
  gone by the **second** generation, 90% by the **third**. Causes: 60% communication breakdown,
  25% unprepared heirs, 15% weak structures. ("Shirtsleeves to shirtsleeves in three generations.")
