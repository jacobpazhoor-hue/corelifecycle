(Hybrid: model knowledge of US special-operations selection/command ladders + widely-reported figures,
July 2026. US framing for concreteness — Army Special Forces "Green Berets" and the tier-1 special
mission units. Ranks/pipelines/attrition are representative and vary by unit, era, and mission.
Non-instructional: this is a dramatized POV of the CAREER and its cost, never real tradecraft how-to.

## The spine on screen: WHAT YOU COMMAND / WHAT YOU COST (a command + attrition STAT, not $)
Like the soldier ladder, special forces is NOT a money story — pay is a modest military salary the whole
way up. The drama is **selection attrition at the bottom, and lives-you-spend at the top**. So the gold
overlay carries a survival/command STAT that escalates by POSITION on the ladder: a roster number and an
attrition rate (you are one of hundreds, most wash out) → the men on your team → the operators in your
troop/squadron → the theaters your task force runs → and, at the apex, the single anonymous SIGNATURE
that approves a target and never appears. The irony the whole episode is built on: you climb from being
*spent* (ammunition — the tip of the spear is the part that breaks off in the wound) to being the one who
*spends* others, and above even you sits a civilian who never trained, never bled, and signs the folder.

## The 8-level ladder (US special-operations framing)
1. **The Candidate — Selection (SFAS / assessment)** — the bottom. You are a name-tape and a roster
   number, no team, no tab. Selection is a multi-week gut-check of rucking, land nav, and sleep
   deprivation designed to make you quit. **Attrition is brutal — routinely well over half wash out**
   (Army SFAS pass rates are often cited around ~30-40%; BUD/S SEAL training attrition is frequently
   reported ~70-85%). Overlay: **ROSTER 47 · MOST QUIT BY DAY THREE**. **VERIFY**: exact pass rates vary
   by class/year and are reported in ranges, not fixed figures.
2. **The Operator — the pipeline + the team (Q Course → ODA)** — you earn the tab / the trident and join
   an operational team. Army Special Forces teams are the **ODA — Operational Detachment Alpha, a 12-man
   detachment** built around 18-series specialties (weapons/engineer/medical/communications/intel). Your
   first deployment: unconventional warfare, foreign internal defense (training partner forces), direct
   action. Overlay: **1 OF 12 · THE TAB — YOU MADE THE TEAM**. **VERIFY**: 12-man ODA is standard Army SF
   doctrine (an ODA "A-Team").
3. **The Team Sergeant / Team Leader** — you run the ODA. Twelve men live or die on your planning and your
   call. This is the senior-NCO or captain heart of Special Forces — the "quiet professionals" doing FID
   and UW far forward with small footprints. Overlay: **12 MEN · THEY DIE ON YOUR CALL**.
4. **Tier One — the Unit (a special mission unit)** — you're selected AGAIN, into a **tier-1 special
   mission unit** (the Army's "Delta"/CAG and the Navy's DEVGRU are the publicly-reported examples) under
   **JSOC (Joint Special Operations Command)**. Its own assessment & selection is even more selective. The
   "**that's real?**" beat: operators here run **sterile — no rank, no unit patches, no name tapes, often
   no ID and civilian grooming — "you don't exist."** Overlay: **NO NAME · YOU DON'T EXIST**. **VERIFY**:
   the existence of Delta/CAG and DEVGRU under JSOC and their sterile/low-profile posture is widely
   reported; specific methods are classified and NOT depicted here.
5. **Troop / Assault Commander** — you stop kicking the door and start planning it. You lead an assault
   troop on **direct action and hostage rescue: HVT (high-value target) raids, the "find-fix-finish"
   cycle**, often multiple objectives a night at the height of a campaign. Overlay: **40 DOORS A NIGHT ·
   HVT #1**. **VERIFY**: high op-tempo (multiple raids/night) is widely reported from Iraq/Afghanistan-era
   JSOC campaigns; exact numbers are unit- and period-specific.
6. **Squadron Commander** — you command a squadron: **100+ operators plus the enablers** (intel, aviation,
   ISR/drones, EOD) across theaters. You now sign for targets instead of clearing rooms. Overlay: **100+
   OPERATORS · YOU SIGN THE TARGET NOW**.
7. **Task Force / JSOC — the joint command** — the command that **spends operators** across the globe: a
   joint task force running the industrial kill/capture chain, ISR feeds, and raids across multiple
   continents. You have become the machine that once spent you. Overlay: **3 CONTINENTS · THE COMMAND THAT
   SPENDS YOU**.
8. **The handlers above (the anonymous apex — the "who signs the folder" beat)** — above the whole special-
   operations enterprise sits the chain that decides IF and WHO: the interagency, the NCA (National Command
   Authority), a case officer / principal who tasks the mission and whose name you are never cleared to
   know. **One signature approves a target; there is no medal, no record, no "you."** You were pointed; you
   never pointed yourself. Overlay: **ONE SIGNATURE · YOU NEVER SEE HIM**.

## Real mechanics / jargon to name (specificity = credibility; non-instructional)
Selection / assessment (SFAS, "the Star Course", BUD/S, Hell Week — named, not detailed), wash-out /
attrition, the ruck, land nav, the Q Course, the tab / the Green Beret / the trident, the **ODA
(A-Team), 18-series MOS**, unconventional warfare (UW), foreign internal defense (FID), direct action
(DA), special reconnaissance, the **kill house / shoot house**, breach / stack / flashbang / the stack on
the door, HVT (high-value target), the **find-fix-finish / F3EA** targeting cycle, ISR (drones/overwatch),
exfil / infil, the bird (helo), sterile kit, tier-1 / special mission unit, **JSOC**, the task force, the
QRF (quick reaction force), MEDEVAC, KIA, the folded flag, dog tags **taped so they don't rattle** (sound
discipline). Above the uniform: the interagency, the case officer, the NCA, "you were never here."

## Emotional arc (the hardening)
earnest candidate who just wants to belong and finish → operator who tapes his tags silent before his
first infil → team sergeant the first time twelve lives ride on his call → the Unit, where you erase your
own name → assault leader who learns to *spend* men for an objective → squadron commander who signs the
target instead of clearing the room → task-force commander who has become the machine that spent his
mentor → and the anonymous apex who never bled and signs the folder. Earnest → exhausted → hardened →
hollow.

## Sensory anchor motif
**Your dog tags, taped together so they don't rattle** — sound discipline, a real detail. New and LOUD at
selection; you tape them the night of your first infil; you check your team's are taped before the bird;
at the Unit you carry NONE (sterile, "you don't exist"); you carry a dead man's tags out at the midpoint;
a drawer of taped tags of men you spent; and at the loop-close a new candidate's tags rattle on a ruck
run — the circle. Secondary senses: the copper/blood taste in combat, gun oil, cordite, rotor wash.
