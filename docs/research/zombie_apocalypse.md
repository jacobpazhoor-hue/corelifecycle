# Research — Could You Survive a Zombie Apocalypse?  (survival format)

Format: **survival** ("Could You Survive [X]?"). The premise itself — a reanimating infection — is
FICTIONAL (the genre's real-world inspiration is rabies: a real bite-transmitted pathogen, ~100% fatal
once symptomatic, with an incubation that can run days to months [VERIFIED, CDC/WHO]). Everything
BUILT AROUND that fictional premise — grid-failure timelines, supply-chain math, triage/martial-law
doctrine, survival physiology, and two real institutional/academic artifacts about this exact scenario —
is REAL and sourced below. This is a DRAMATIZED cautionary POV, not a how-to.

Second-person, present-tense POV: you are an ordinary suburban parent (spouse MARA, son DEC, 8) when
the outbreak reaches your street on an otherwise normal Tuesday. The "levels" are a MONOTONIC TIME/
THREAT ladder (HOUR 0 → HOUR 1 → DAY 1 → DAY 3 → WEEK 1 → WEEK 3 → MONTH 2 → YEAR 1), each stage a
worse threat, not a job rank. The gold overlay becomes a **survival stat** at each stage (a headcount,
a day count, an odds figure, a distance) while the color grade escalates by level POSITION regardless
of the mixed units — same engine as could_you_survive_ocean.md.

## The threat ladder (the on-screen spine) — all REAL mechanics unless FLAGGED

### HOUR 0 — THE OUTBREAK (cold open, unplaced in time) (overlay: "0.4 MI")
- Opens mid-chase, already inside the horde — resolved later as how HOUR 0 actually started.
- Real institutional artifact (share beat #1): in **May 2011**, the **CDC** published a real blog post
  and preparedness guide, *"Preparedness 101: Zombie Apocalypse,"* using a fictional outbreak to teach
  genuine disaster-readiness (go-bags, water storage, family communication plans) — traffic to CDC's
  emergency-preparedness site spiked so hard the campaign is cited as a case study in public-health
  communication. **REAL, verifiable, still archived.**

### HOUR 1 — THE BITE NEXT DOOR  (overlay: "1 BITE")
- The genre's real inspiration is **rabies**: transmitted by a bite/saliva, attacks the nervous system,
  and is considered **essentially 100% fatal once clinical symptoms begin** (the reason exposure
  prophylaxis has to happen BEFORE symptoms) — incubation is typically **2–12 weeks** but documented
  cases range from days to over a year depending on bite location and viral load. **REAL, CDC/WHO.**
  A close-contact bite is the fictional accelerant here (fictional: an incubation compressed to hours).
- **Normalcy bias**: documented disaster psychology — most people underreact to the first sign of a
  real threat (fire alarms ignored, evacuation orders delayed) because the brain defaults to "this is
  probably fine." **REAL, well-documented pattern** (e.g., 9/11 Twin Towers evacuation delays studied
  by NIST). Used for the beat where you almost don't act on the neighbor's door.

### DAY 1 — THE EVACUATION / THE GRID STILL UP  (overlay: "3 DAYS")
- **The "3-day supply chain"**: U.S. grocery stores and distribution centers run on **just-in-time
  inventory**, commonly cited at roughly **3 days of on-shelf stock** for a given store absent
  resupply — a widely repeated supply-chain/FEMA-preparedness figure (hence FEMA's own "72-hour kit"
  guidance). FLAG: an industry rule of thumb, not a single universal audited number, but consistently
  cited across logistics and emergency-management sources. **Real-world grounding for how fast shelves
  actually empty.**
- Real disaster analog for evacuation gridlock: **Hurricane Rita (2005)**, evacuating ~2.5–3 million
  people from the Houston area, produced a **100+ mile traffic jam** with some drivers taking **20+
  hours** to travel roughts normally under 3 — the real reference for the highway-parking-lot beat.
  **REAL, widely reported.**

### DAY 3 — THE STORE RUN / SOCIAL BREAKDOWN  (overlay: "72 HRS")
- **The "72-hour" collapse window**: FEMA and emergency-management literature repeatedly cite roughly
  **72 hours** as the point at which, absent restored order/supply, a disrupted community's social
  contract visibly frays (looting, self-organized violence) — the real analog is the documented
  breakdown timeline inside the New Orleans Superdome/Convention Center after **Hurricane Katrina
  (2005)**, where conditions inside deteriorated sharply within the first **3–4 days**. **REAL,
  documented, though causes were disputed/exaggerated in initial reporting — the general "days, not
  weeks" collapse timeline is the well-sourced part.**
- **Rule of threes** (reused from ocean/survival literature): ~3 minutes without air, ~**3 days
  without water**, ~3 weeks without food — an approximate survival heuristic, not a hard limit. FLAG:
  varies with heat, exertion, health. **REAL rule of thumb.**

### WEEK 1 — THE BARRICADE  (overlay: "1 GAL")
- Real water math: **FEMA/CDC guidance recommends ~1 gallon of water per person per day** (drinking +
  basic hygiene); a gallon weighs **8.34 lb**, so a 4-person household's 2-week reserve is **~233 lb**
  of water alone — the real logistics behind "you can't carry your way out of this." **REAL, FEMA/CDC.**
- Real home-defense/fortification doctrine (documented in disaster-prep and even structural-engineering
  literature on "hardening" a home against forced entry): reinforcing door frames at the strike plate
  and boarding windows meaningfully slows, but does not stop, sustained forced entry — a real, sourced
  fact used honestly (the barricade buys time, not safety). **REAL.**

### WEEK 3 — THE BITE INSIDE THE WALLS (the midpoint reversal)  (overlay: "12 HRS")
- The real rabies-incubation science above is compressed here (FICTIONAL acceleration) into a
  household countdown: once bitten, symptoms are inevitable and irreversible — the emotional core of
  the reversal is real triage ethics, not fictional physiology: **military and disaster-medicine
  "black tag" triage protocol** (START/SALT systems) REQUIRES designating patients as expectant/
  unsalvageable when treating them would cost resources needed to save others — a real, still-used,
  ethically brutal military and mass-casualty medical framework. **REAL, documented (START triage,
  1983, Hoag Hospital/Newport Beach Fire Dept, still standard in U.S. EMS mass-casualty response).**

### MONTH 2 — THE CHECKPOINT  (overlay: "1 IN 4")
- Real martial-law/continuity mechanism: under the U.S. **Insurrection Act** and state emergency-powers
  statutes, a governor (or in extremis the President) can federalize/deploy the **National Guard** for
  domestic order enforcement, checkpoints, and curfews — the real legal machinery behind "the checkpoint
  that decides who gets in." **REAL, used in real disasters (Katrina, LA riots 1992).**
  the checkpoint's brutal filter (a fictional "1 in 4 bitten by exposure" screening rate is dramatized,
  grounded in real triage math, not a cited outbreak statistic — FLAG as dramatized).

### YEAR 1 — THE WALLED CAMP (the apex, at cost)  (overlay: "40 ACRES")
- Real subsistence-farming math: intensive small-scale/biointensive agriculture estimates commonly cite
  roughly **1/4 to 1 acre of well-worked land per person** to approach food self-sufficiency depending
  on method and climate — the real number behind why the camp's walls only enclose so much land and no
  more. FLAG: estimates vary widely by soil/climate/technique; used here as an order-of-magnitude, not
  a precise citation. **Grounded, approximate.**
- **Share-worthy "wait, that's real?" beat (the anchor fact of the whole episode):** a real, peer-
  reviewed academic paper — Munz, Hudea, Imad & Smith, *"When Zombies Attack!: Mathematical Modelling
  of an Outbreak of Zombie Infection"* (University of Ottawa/Carleton, published in *Infectious Disease
  Modelling Research Progress*, 2009) — used a real SIR-style epidemiological differential-equation
  model (borrowed from actual outbreak science) and concluded that without an **immediate, overwhelming,
  and sustained** counter-response, a zombie-style outbreak mathematically overwhelms and ends
  civilization within a matter of months. **REAL, peer-reviewed, widely cited/taught as an epidemiology
  teaching tool** — the paper's real point is that the MATH of exponential contagion, not the fiction,
  is what should scare you (used verbatim as the episode's closing "wait, that's real?" beat).

## Pop-culture grounding (real film-history facts, used honestly, not as unlicensed IP)
- **George Romero's *Night of the Living Dead* (1968)** established the slow, shambling "zombie" as a
  genre archetype — DELIBERATELY slow, because the horror was inevitability and numbers, not speed.
  **Sprinting zombies are a later invention**, popularized by *28 Days Later* (2002) and the *Dawn of
  the Dead* (2004) remake. **REAL film history**, used as the "wait, that's real?" secondary beat
  explaining why THIS episode's horde is slow-but-overwhelming (numbers, not speed, is the real threat
  math from the Ottawa paper above).
