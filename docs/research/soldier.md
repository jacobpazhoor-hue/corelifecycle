## The spine on screen: TROOPS UNDER YOUR COMMAND (power) + your pay (sub-caption)
A soldier's escalation is NOT money — pay rises only modestly and CAPS near the top. The drama is the
INVERSE of the finance ladders: power over human lives explodes while pay stays small and flat. So the
big gold number is **how many soldiers' lives you command**; the **sub-caption** carries your modest pay.
This sharpens the central irony: by the top you command hundreds of thousands of lives for a civil-
servant salary — and above even you sit civilians who have never fired a shot and decide where you point.
Mirrors the surgeon/hedge non-personal apex: open on the system's scale, end on "they decide what a war
is worth." (improvements.json: non-finance-spine-validated.)

## The 8-level ladder + numbers (US Army framing)
1. **Private / Recruit (E-1 → E-3)** — the bottom. You command **no one**; you ARE the unit, one rifle in
   a formation. Boot camp strips the civilian out of you. Base pay **~$24K–$26K/yr** (E-1 base ~$2,017/mo
   2024, ~$24.2K; +housing/food allowances on top). Overlay: **0 UNDER YOUR COMMAND · ~$25K**.
2. **Sergeant / NCO (E-5 → E-6)** — the backbone. You lead a **squad (~9 soldiers)** — the first lives that
   are yours. NCOs run the day-to-day; "the backbone of the Army." All-in **~$45K–$55K**. Overlay:
   **9 UNDER YOUR COMMAND · ~$50K**.
3. **Lieutenant → Captain (O-1 → O-3)** — first commission. Lead a **platoon (~40)**, then command a
   **company (~150)**. The first time you SIGN for lives — order people toward fire. Pay **~$60K–$95K**.
   Overlay: **~150 UNDER YOUR COMMAND · ~$90K**.
4. **Major → Lt. Colonel (O-4 → O-5)** — field grade. You stop pulling triggers and start PLANNING; a
   Lt. Colonel commands a **battalion (~700)**. You spend lives for objectives on a map. Pay **~$110K–$140K**.
   Overlay: **~700 UNDER YOUR COMMAND · ~$130K**.
5. **Colonel (O-6)** — command a **brigade (~4,500)**, a self-contained fighting force with its own armor,
   artillery, logistics. You think in operations, not engagements. Pay **~$150K–$165K**. Overlay:
   **~4,500 UNDER YOUR COMMAND · ~$160K**.
6. **General — 1-to-3-star (O-7 → O-9)** — flag officer. Command a **division (~15,000)** up to a
   **corps (~45,000)**. You think in "acceptable losses" and strategy; politics enters the job. Pay
   **~$190K–$200K** (approaching the cap). Overlay: **~20,000 UNDER YOUR COMMAND · ~$190K**.
7. **4-Star General / Combatant Commander (O-10)** — the apex of the uniform. A theater/combatant command
   or service chief: **hundreds of thousands** of troops, nuclear-capable forces, whole regions of the
   globe. You advise the President; you EXECUTE wars you do not declare. And the pay is **CAPPED** at
   Executive Schedule Level II — **~$221K/yr** (2024 cap $18,491.70/mo). You command armies for a senior-
   bureaucrat salary. Overlay: **~200,000 UNDER YOUR COMMAND · ~$221K (CAPPED)**.
8. **The civilians who order them (the "Architect" beat)** — above every general sits the chain that
   decides IF and WHERE: the Secretary of Defense, the Commander-in-Chief, and Congress, which holds the
   war power and the purse — a **~$895B** US defense budget (FY25 NDAA total national defense), a slice of
   **~$2.7 trillion** in global military spending. They have never fired a shot. They decide what a war is
   worth. The soldier is pointed; he does not point himself. Overlay: **$895,000,000,000 · THE DEFENSE
   BUDGET** then **$2,700,000,000,000 · SPENT ON WAR EACH YEAR**.

## Emotional arc (the hardening, military flavor)
earnest recruit who wants to belong → NCO who hardens the next recruits the way he was hardened → young
officer the first time he signs an order that sends people toward fire → field officer who learns to
spend lives for a line on a map → general who says "acceptable losses" without flinching → 4-star who
executes a war he privately doubts because that is the oath → and the apex who never bleeds and decides
it all.
