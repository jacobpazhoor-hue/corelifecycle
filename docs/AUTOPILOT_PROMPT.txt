   POV SENTENCE CRAFT (hard rules):
     • PRESENT TENSE always ("You walk in," never "walked").
     • Break "you…you…you" drone with the IMPERATIVE ("Don't look back. Count the doors.").
     • Body-based dread — state the FACT, not the feeling: "Your hands are steady. That's the part
       that scares you." (never "you feel scared").
     • BAN PERCEPTION FILTERS — never "you see / you notice / you feel / you watch / you realize".
       Describe the world DIRECTLY ("The ledger doesn't balance.") — the "you" is assumed. Filters
       put the viewer OVER the character's shoulder instead of inside them (DM-craft finding).
     • 2–3 SENSES per scene, favoring sound + smell as the second impression (not just sight).
     • SENSORY ANCHOR MOTIF — pick ONE recurring physical sensation/object for the whole episode (the
       ring on your finger, the smell of gun oil, the weight of the envelope) and RE-TRIGGER it at
       every level-up. It becomes a callback engine and the through-line the viewer feels tighten.
     • SPECIFICITY over abstraction: "Forty-two hundred in a cereal box," not "good money." Exact
       numbers, named people, concrete objects.
     • ANAPHORA at theme moments (repeat a stem across 3 lines: "Money buys medicine. Money buys
       safety. Money buys a way out.").
     • Sentence-length VARIATION: average under 15 words; drop to 2–4 word punches at emotional peaks.
     • A NAMED MENTOR/rival introduced early (sets up a later betrayal/loss payoff).
     • Plant ONE share-worthy "wait — that's real?" beat (a shocking true fact/number) — shares are
       weighted ~5–8× likes.
